The Sharpening Project

Mentor Rules &
Protocols

Quick Reference Card — This does not replace the full manual. You are expected to read and follow the full document.

Section 1

Big Picture

  • This is a Christ-centered mentoring program.
  • Parents are the primary leaders of their sons.
  • Safety > convenience every time.
  • Tennessee law: everyone is a mandatory reporter of suspected abuse or neglect.
Section 2

Before You Meet

You may only mentor if:
  • You are an approved mentor (application, signed waiver).
  • The student's parent has turned in a signed Consent form.
  • Parents know: who, where, when, and how long.
Location Rules
  • Public place (park, coffee shop, restaurant), OR
  • Private home only with written parent consent + another adult present.
  • No closed bedrooms, locked rooms, or isolated spaces.
Transportation
  • Only with written parent permission.
  • Valid driver's license + insurance required.
  • Seatbelts always. Advance permission for multiple students.
Section 3

Communication Rules

  • Assume everything can be read by parents and leadership.
  • Respectful, appropriate, Christ-honoring — always.
  • No romantic or flirty messages.
  • No late-night conversations (aim for 7 AM – 10 PM).
  • Use group chats with parents when possible.
If They Ask for Secrecy
"I care about you too much to keep anything that could hurt you or someone else a secret."
Section 4

Social Media & Photos

  • No photos of students without signed media consent.
  • No social media DMs with students — treat same as texting.
  • Never share a student's story or private messages publicly.
  • Do not tag students under 18 without explicit parent permission.
Section 5

Spiritual Standards

  • Walk with Jesus consistently in biblical faith.
  • Avoid profanity, pornography, drunkenness, drug use, illegal activity.
  • If personally struggling with something affecting your mentoring — tell leadership.
  • Keep phones away during meetings; come prayed up.
Dating & Sexuality Topics
  • Listen → Point to Scripture → Encourage talking to parents → Involve leadership if unsafe.
Section 6

Hard Topics (Non-Emergency)

Tell leadership immediately if a student shares:

  • Porn or sexual addiction
  • Ongoing anxiety, depression, or disordered eating
  • Substance use
  • Serious family crisis
  • Identity struggles or intense shame
  • Anything that feels too big for you
How to Share
  • Short and factual: what they said, when, any obvious risk.
  • Avoid gossip. Protect dignity. Don't hide real issues.
Section 7

Self-Harm & Suicide

Immediate Steps
  • Stay calm. Listen.
  • "I am really glad you told me. You are not alone in this."
  • Ask: How often? Have you made a plan? What makes it worse? Do your parents know?
  • You may read Psalm 139 and pray with them.
Reporting
  • Inform parents as soon as possible.
  • Inform Program Director and Head Advisor.
  • If immediate danger → call 911.
  • Complete a written incident report within 24 hours.
Section 8

Mandatory Reporting

Report immediately if you suspect:

  • Physical, sexual, or emotional abuse
  • Neglect (basic needs not met)
  • Child witnessing abuse/domestic violence
  • Self-harm or suicidal intent
  • Serious threats to harm someone else
TN Child Abuse Hotline
1-877-237-0004

Telling leadership does NOT replace your legal duty to report to civil authorities.

Section 9

Never Do These

Sexual contact, comments, or romantic behavior toward a student

Emotionally dependent or "exclusive" relationship

Physical discipline of any kind

Share sexual stories, explicit jokes, or pornography

Use or provide drugs, alcohol, or vapes near students

Harass, bully, or verbally attack students

Section 10

Events & Documentation

Events Must Have
  • Clear start and end times
  • Adequate adult supervision
  • Open, public spaces when possible
  • Attendance record (mentors + students)
Required Forms
  • Students: Signed Parent Consent form
  • Mentors: Application + Credibility Statement + Commitment Waiver
Incident Reports Required For
  • Suspected abuse or neglect
  • Self-harm or suicidal talk
  • Major behavioral incidents
Section 11 — Final Reminder

If You Are Ever Unsure

You are not expected to handle things alone. Contact the Program Director, Head Advisor, or Lead Mentor Coordinator. When in doubt about safety, report. You are not expected to be a counselor. You are expected to be honest, humble, careful, and obedient to God and to the law.
