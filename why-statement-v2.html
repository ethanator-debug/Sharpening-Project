Why We Exist
A generation of young men is growing up
disconnected — from purpose, from truth,
and from the One who made them.
The Sharpening Project exists to change that.

Our movement was created with one conviction:

When a young man knows who he is in Christ,
he becomes unshakable.

We exist to build a Jesus-centered brotherhood where young men can be honest without fear, seen without judgment, and led toward the kind of transformation that lasts a lifetime. Every mentorship relationship, every hangout, and every conversation exists so young men can:

  • Experience the love of God through trusted mentors
  • Grow into leaders who bring light into dark places
  • Step into freedom from temptation, shame, and spiritual confusion
  • Walk boldly in the purpose God designed them for
This is not just a program —
This is a generation-wide discipleship movement.
A brotherhood sharpening brotherhood.
Our Mission

Three Core Goals

These three goals define everything the Sharpening Project does.

If an event, action, or decision doesn't support these three goals — we don't do it.

Goal 01
Loved & Safe

To make sure every student feels deeply loved, protected, and supported — never walking through life alone.

A young man grows fastest in a place where he feels safe enough to be honest. We exist to create that place.

Goal 02
Lead with Strength

To raise up young men who model: faith, humility, courage, integrity, and Christ-centered leadership.

Mentors don't just guide students — they become the kind of men students one day want to become.

We win when students become mentors themselves.

Goal 03
Lasting Freedom

To guide young men toward real, long-term freedom — freedom rooted in truth, Scripture, accountability, and the Holy Spirit.

Freedom from temptation. Freedom from isolation. Freedom from the lies that keep young men stuck.

Our goal is discipleship that changes futures — not just behavior.

Everything Flows From This
Every Event
Every Mentor
Every Meeting
Every Decision

—must advance love, leadership, or freedom.

If not, it doesn't belong in this movement.

Jesus stays at the center. Always.
